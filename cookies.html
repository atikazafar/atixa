Cookie Policy

This policy explains what cookies are, which ones we use on Atixa, why we use them, and how you can control them.

📅 Last updated: January 1, 2026 ✅ GDPR & ePrivacy compliant

Summary: We use a small number of cookies — some essential for the site to work, and some for analytics and advertising (Google AdSense). You can opt out of non-essential cookies at any time.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to personalise your experience, and to provide information to site owners about how their site is being used.

Cookies can be "session cookies" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (stored on your device for a set period or until you delete them). They can be "first-party" (set by the website you are visiting) or "third-party" (set by another organisation via the site).

These cookies are necessary for the website to function. They cannot be switched off.

2.3 Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics to help us understand how visitors use our Site. All data collected is anonymised and aggregated. No personally identifiable information is collected through these cookies.

Cookie NameTypePurposeDuration
_ga Analytics Registers a unique ID used to generate statistical data on how visitors use the Site. IP is anonymised. 2 years

_gid Analytics Registers a unique ID used to generate statistical data on how the visitor uses the Site. 24 hours
_gat Analytics Used by Google Tag Manager to throttle request rate. 1 minute

To opt out of Google Analytics, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

2.4 Advertising Cookies (Google AdSense)

We display advertisements through Google AdSense. Google uses cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our Site and other sites across the internet. These cookies enable Google and its partners to personalise the ads you see.

Cookie NameTypePurposeDuration
IDE Advertising Used by Google DoubleClick to register and report the user's actions after viewing or clicking an advertisement. Enables measuring ad effectiveness. 1 year
test_cookie Advertising Used to check if the user's browser supports cookies. 15 minutes
DSID Advertising Used to identify a signed-in Google user when accessing Google products from a non-Google site. 2 weeks
NID Advertising Stores user preferences and information for Google services, including personalised ads. 6 months

To opt out of personalised advertising from Google, visit Google Ads Settings. You can also opt out of interest-based advertising from multiple companies at once via aboutads.info or youronlinechoices.eu (EU).

3. How to Control Cookies

You have several options for controlling cookies:

  • Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies through their settings. Note that blocking all cookies will affect the functionality of many websites, including Atixa (your theme preference will not be saved).
  • Google opt-out: Use the tools listed in section 2.4 above to opt out of Google's advertising and analytics cookies.
  • Industry opt-out tools: Visit aboutads.info (US) or youronlinechoices.eu (EU) to manage preferences across multiple advertisers.

Browser-specific cookie management guides:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

4. Cookies & GDPR

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA) and the United Kingdom, we rely on your consent as the legal basis for placing non-essential cookies (analytics and advertising). You have the right to withdraw consent at any time by using the opt-out methods described above.

We do not use cookies to collect special category data (sensitive personal information) as defined under the GDPR.

5. Google Fonts

Our Site uses Google Fonts for typography. When your browser requests the font files, Google may log your IP address. This is considered a standard technical request. For more information, see Google's Privacy Policy. If you prefer, you can disable Google Fonts in your browser by blocking requests to fonts.googleapis.com.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in the cookies we use or for legal or regulatory reasons. Updated versions will be posted on this page with a revised date.
